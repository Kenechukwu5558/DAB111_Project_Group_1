Source of Data:

The Data used for this project was gotten from Kaggle. A full view of the utilized data can be found here

Context:

The growth of supermarkets in most populated cities are increasing and market competitions are also high. The dataset is one of the historical sales of supermarket company which has recorded in 3 different branches for 3 months data. Predictive data analytics methods are easy to apply with this dataset.


Variable Definitions:

Variable NameData TypeDescription
Invoice ID categorical Computer generated sales slip invoice identification number. Example: 750-67-8428
Branch categorical Branch of supercenter (3 branches are available identified by A, B and C).
City categorical Location of supercenters. Example: Yangon, Mandalay
Customer type categorical Type of customers, recorded by Members for customers using member card and Normal for without member card.
Gender categorical Gender type of customer. Example:Male, Female
Product line categorical General item categorization groups - Electronic accessories, Fashion accessories, Food and beverages, Health and beauty, Home and lifestyle, Sports and travel
Unit price continuous Price of each product in $ (dolars) from 10.08 to 99.96
Quantity continuous Number of products purchased by customer from 1 to 10
Tax 5% continuous 5% tax fee for customer buying from 0.5085 to 49.65
Total continuous Total price including tax from 10.6785 to 1042.65
Date categorical Date of purchase (Record available from January 2019 to March 2019). Example: 1/5/2019
Time continuous Purchase time (10am to 9pm) from 10:00 to 20:59
Payment categorical Payment used by customer for purchase (3 methods are available â€“ Cash, Credit card and Ewallet). Example: Ewallet, Cash
COGS continuous Cost of goods sold from 10.17 to 993
Gross Margin Percentage continuous Gross margin percentage. Example:4.761904762
Gross Income continuous Gross income from 0.5085 to 49.65
Rating categorical Customer stratification rating on their overall shopping experience (On a scale of 1 to 10)
